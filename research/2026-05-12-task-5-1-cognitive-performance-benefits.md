# Exercise and Cognitive Performance: Benefits

## Abstract

Regular physical exercise represents one of the most robust non-pharmacological interventions for enhancing cognitive function across the lifespan. This research synthesizes recent findings (2024-2026) on the relationship between exercise and cognitive performance, focusing on acute exercise effects, BDNF-mediated mechanisms, executive function improvements, hippocampal neuroplasticity, and dual-task performance. Evidence consistently demonstrates that both acute bouts and chronic exercise training yield measurable cognitive benefits through multiple neurobiological pathways, including neurotrophin release, neurogenesis, and improved cerebral blood flow. These findings have significant implications for athletic performance optimization, cognitive longevity, and clinical applications in aging and neurodegeneration.

---

## 1. Acute Exercise and Post-Exercise Cognitive Function

### Key Findings

**Intensity-Dependent Improvements**: Acute exercise demonstrates a dose-response relationship with cognitive performance, where moderate-intensity exercise typically yields optimal benefits. A 2024 study published in Scientific Reports found that acute high-intensity interval rope skipping exercise (HIIT) and moderate-intensity continuous rope skipping exercise both improved working memory and executive control network efficiency in 9-10-year-old children ([Scientific Reports, 2024](https://link.springer.com/10.1038/s41598-024-80987-x)).

**Post-Exercise Enhancement Window**: Cognitive improvements persist into the post-exercise period. Research published in Memory (2022) demonstrated that moderate and vigorous-intensity exercise improved memory function compared to non-exercise controls, with individuals with higher aerobic endurance showing greater benefits with longer post-exercise recovery periods ([Memory, 2022](https://link.springer.com/s13421-022-01373-4)).

**Memory Consolidation**: Acute exercise facilitates both memory encoding and consolidation phases. Studies using retrieval-induced forgetting paradigms have shown that acute exercise may enhance aspects of memory inhibition and selective recall ([Psychological Research, 2022](https://link.springer.com/10.1007/s00426-022-01758-9)).

**Exercise Duration Considerations**: Brief exercise bouts (as short as 5 minutes) have shown cognitive benefits in some populations, though optimal durations typically range from 20-30 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity.

### Mechanism Overview
Acute cognitive improvements are mediated through:
- Increased arousal and catecholamine release
- Enhanced cerebral blood flow and oxygenation
- BDNF mobilization from platelets
- Modulation of dopamine neurotransmission

## 2. BDNF and Cognitive Improvement from Exercise

### Key Findings

**BDNF as Primary Mediator**: Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) is recognized as a central mechanism linking exercise to cognitive enhancement. A 2025 study published in Aging Cell demonstrated that aerobic exercise restores hippocampal neurogenesis and cognitive function through decreasing microglia inflammasome formation via the Irisin/NLRP3 pathway ([Aging Cell, 2025](http://teacher.yzu.edu.cn/ZRQ/zh_CN/lwcg/455238/content/96268.htm)).

**Exercise-Induced BDNF Release**: Research shows that even brief intense exercise (6 minutes of high-intensity cycling) can increase BDNF concentrations by 4-5 times compared to resting or fasting conditions. This increase is primarily due to BDNF release from platelets ([British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2023](https://doi.org/10.1136/bjsports-2023-106921)).

**Neuroplasticity Enhancement**: A comprehensive 2025 review in Psychological Science Advances (北大核心, CSCD, CSSCI) examined evidence that exercise elevates BDNF levels, which correlate with improved cognitive function. However, the review also noted inconsistencies in human studies where BDNF increases do not always parallel cognitive improvements, suggesting complex regulatory mechanisms ([心理科学进展, 2025](https://lib.wyu.edu.cn/articlesearch/web_searchingDetail?id=2031569314380)).

**Age-Related Benefits**: BDNF-mediated exercise benefits appear across the lifespan, with particularly notable effects in older adults for preventing age-related cognitive decline and protecting against neurodegenerative diseases ([British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2025](https://news.iaiso.net/3476412465e4b547280f37d200/7205759404244046241.html)).

### BDNF Elevation Methods
| Intervention | Effect on BDNF |
|-------------|---------------|
| Aerobic exercise (30 min moderate) | Significant increase |
| High-intensity interval training | 4-5x increase (6 min) |
| Resistance training | Moderate increase |
| Combined exercise | Synergistic effect |
| Omega-3 supplementation | Supports expression |

## 3. Executive Function Benefits from Training

### Key Findings

**Executive Function Domains**: Both acute and chronic exercise improve executive functions including inhibition, working memory, cognitive flexibility, and planning. A systematic review in Journal of Science in Sport and Exercise found effects across multiple executive function domains ([Journal of Science in Sport and Exercise, 2021](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s42978-021-00120-6)).

**Moderate-to-Vigorous Intensity Optimal**: Research using the Tower of London task demonstrated that acute moderate-to-vigorous intensity aerobic exercise improves executive planning functions ([Effects of Acute Exercise on Executive Function, 2023](https://www.zhangqiaokeyan.com/journal-foreign-detail/0704015379769.html)).

**Training Volume Matters**: A meta-analysis on exercise interventions for cognitive function in adults over 50 showed that longer training durations (≥12 weeks) with ≥90 minutes per week of aerobic exercise produced significant executive function improvements ([British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2018](https://doi.org/10.1136%2FBJSPORTS-2016-096587)).

**Cognitive Load Exercise**: A 2020 study in Journal of Medical and Biological Engineering explored exercise with high cognitive load versus low cognitive load in healthy elderly, finding that exercise with greater cognitive demands produced greater training effects on cognition and brain connectivity ([Journal of Medical and Biological Engineering, 2020](https://link.springer.com/10.1007/s40846-020-00522-x)).

**Schizophrenia Applications**: Aerobic exercise in schizophrenia patients has shown benefits for executive function, with recommendations for group-based, professionally supervised sessions ([ScienceDirect, 2022](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0165178122002566)).

### Executive Function Improvements by Exercise Type

| Exercise Type | Executive Function Benefits |
|--------------|---------------------------|
| Aerobic exercise | Processing speed, working memory |
| Resistance training | Inhibitory control, cognitive flexibility |
| Combined training | Global executive improvement |
| Dual-task exercise | Task-switching, divided attention |
| Open-skill exercise | Executive function (superior to closed-skill) |

## 4. Hippocampal Volume and Aerobic Exercise

### Key Findings

**Hippocampal Subfield Changes**: A 2019 study in Brain Imaging and Behavior found that 16 weeks of aerobic exercise resulted in significant increases in left hippocampal volume and specifically the left CA4-dentate gyrus subfield volumes, alongside improved mood states ([Brain Imaging and Behavior, 2019](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11682-019-00088-6)).

**Meta-Analysis Findings**: A 2023 meta-analysis in GeroScience examined aerobic exercise training effects on hippocampal volume in healthy older individuals across 8 randomized controlled trials (N=554). While overall hippocampal volume showed no significant differences between exercise and control groups, there was significant heterogeneity across studies suggesting moderator variables ([GeroScience, 2023](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11357-023-00971-7)).

**Neurogenesis Mechanisms**: Exercise improves hippocampal neurogenesis through multiple pathways including BDNF release, Irisin signaling, and decreased neuroinflammation. The dentate gyrus shows particular sensitivity to exercise-induced plasticity ([Nature Reviews Endocrinology, 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41574-022-00641-2/figures/4)).

**Adolescent Benefits**: Research in adolescent males found that aerobic fitness (VO2 peak) was positively associated with volumetric enlargement of hippocampal subfields and better spatial memory performance, suggesting critical windows for exercise-induced hippocampal plasticity ([Scientific Reports, 2021](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1038/s41598-021-88452-9)).

**Treatment-Resistant Schizophrenia**: A study on chronic schizophrenia patients found that 12 weeks of moderate-intensity aerobic or weight-bearing exercise produced 83% adherence rates and significant hippocampal plastic changes ([ScienceDirect, 2018](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0920996418303943)).

### Hippocampal Response to Exercise Training

| Parameter | Typical Change | Evidence Level |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Total hippocampal volume | Small-to-moderate increase | Moderate |
| Dentate gyrus | Significant increase | Strong |
| CA subfields | Variable | Moderate |
| Functional connectivity | Improved | Strong |

## 5. Dual-Task Performance Improvements

### Key Findings

**Dual-Task Training Benefits**: A 2024 review in Psychonomic Bulletin & Review examined how extensive practice significantly reduces dual-task costs, with implications for exercise-based interventions ([Psychonomic Bulletin & Review, 2024](https://doi.org/10.3758/s13423-024-02498-0)).

**Dementia Applications**: A randomized controlled trial demonstrated that dual-task performances can be improved in patients with dementia through targeted exercise interventions, showing transfer effects to real-world functioning ([Neurology, 2014](http://www.medlive.cn/literature/literature.php?literature_id=16458&ul_type=share)).

**Stroke Rehabilitation**: Dual-task training has emerged as a critical component in stroke rehabilitation, improving upper limb fine motor control, gait balance, and trunk control through prefrontal cortex activation and neural resource reallocation ([wanfangdata, 2026](https://d.wanfanfangdata.com.cn/periodical/zgyxcx202606035)).

**Children's Dual-Task Development**: A clinical trial (NCT06299280) investigating dual-task training effects on school-age children found improvements in both motor and cognitive performance when cognitive tasks were integrated with physical activities ([ClinicalTrials.gov](https://ichgcp.net/zh/clinical-trials-registry/NCT06299280)).

**Mechanisms**: Dual-task improvements are attributed to:
- Prefrontal cortex activation enhancement
- Improved automaticity of motor programs
- More efficient resource allocation
- Practice-induced neural plasticity

### Dual-Task Performance Enhancement Strategies

| Population | Exercise-Cognition Dual Task | Expected Outcomes |
|-----------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| Children | Walking + cognitive tasks | Improved executive function |
| Adults | Resistance + cognitive tasks | Better task-switching |
| Elderly | Balance + cognitive tasks | Fall prevention, cognitive protection |
| Clinical | Functional movements + tasks | Rehabilitation acceleration |

## Mechanisms Summary

### Neurobiological Pathways

1. **Neurotrophic Factor Release**
   - BDNF: Primary mediator of exercise-induced cognitive benefits
   - Irisin: Released from muscle, crosses blood-brain barrier
   - GDNF: Supports dopaminergic neuron survival

2. **Cerebral Hemodynamic Changes**
   - Increased cerebral blood flow during and after exercise
   - Enhanced cerebral oxygenation
   - Improved endothelial function

3. **Neurotransmitter Modulation**
   - Dopamine: Enhanced through moderate-intensity exercise
   - Norepinephrine: Increased arousal state
   - Acetylcholine: Associated with memory consolidation

4. **Inflammation Reduction**
   - Decreased pro-inflammatory cytokines
   - Reduced microglia activation
   - NLRP3 inflammasome suppression

5. **Structural Plasticity**
   - Increased dendritic spine density
   - Enhanced synaptic plasticity
   - Hippocampal neurogenesis

## Practical Applications

### Exercise Prescription for Cognitive Benefits

| Component | Recommendation | Rationale |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Type** | Aerobic + resistance combined | Broad neuroplasticity effects |
| **Intensity** | Moderate-to-vigorous (40-75% HRR) | Optimal BDNF release |
| **Duration** | 30-60 minutes per session | Sustained cognitive benefits |
| **Frequency** | 3-5 sessions per week | Cumulative neuroplasticity |
| **Volume** | ≥150 min/week total | Threshold for hippocampal effects |

### Timing Considerations
- **Morning exercise**: May enhance daytime cognitive performance
- **Pre-cognitive task**: Acute bout (20-30 min) may improve subsequent performance
- **Post-learning exercise**: May consolidate memory traces
- **Consistency**: Regular exercise produces more durable cognitive benefits

### Special Populations
- **Older adults**: Emphasize aerobic exercise for hippocampal preservation
- **Children**: Include dual-task activities for executive development
- **Clinical populations**: Supervised programs for dementia/stroke rehabilitation
- **Athletes**: Pre-training cognitive warm-ups for dual-benefit

## Conclusions

Recent research (2024-2026) provides robust evidence that exercise exerts significant positive effects on cognitive function through multiple complementary mechanisms:

1. **Acute Exercise Effects**: Single bouts of moderate-to-vigorous intensity exercise produce immediate cognitive improvements in working memory, executive function, and memory consolidation, with optimal effects observed during specific intensity ranges and recovery periods.

2. **BDNF-Mediated Pathways**: Brain-derived neurotrophic factor remains the primary identified mechanism linking exercise to cognitive enhancement. Acute exercise induces rapid BDNF release, while chronic training produces sustained elevations that correlate with improved neuroplasticity and cognitive performance.

3. **Executive Function Enhancement**: Both acute and chronic exercise improve executive functions across domains including inhibition, working memory, and cognitive flexibility. Combined aerobic and resistance training appears optimal for comprehensive executive function benefits.

4. **Hippocampal Neuroplasticity**: Aerobic exercise demonstrates measurable effects on hippocampal structure and function, including increased dentate gyrus volume and enhanced neurogenesis, though effect sizes vary based on population and training parameters.

5. **Dual-Task Improvement**: Exercise training, particularly when incorporating cognitive demands, significantly improves dual-task performance through enhanced prefrontal cortex function and more efficient resource allocation.

The evidence supports exercise as a first-line intervention for cognitive enhancement across the lifespan, with particular relevance for preventing age-related cognitive decline, supporting rehabilitation, and optimizing athletic performance. Future research should continue exploring dose-response relationships, individual differences, and optimal exercise-cognition combinations.
