# Exercise and Cardiac Risk: Sudden Cardiac Death and Arrhythmias in Athletes

## Abstract

Sudden cardiac death (SCD) remains the leading medical cause of death in athletes, though the precise incidence varies widely depending on age, sex, race, and sport type. This research examines recent studies (2024-2026) on cardiac risk factors in athletes, including SCD epidemiology, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and exercise recommendations, long QT syndrome (LQTS) management, exercise-induced arrhythmias, and screening protocols. Evidence demonstrates a paradigm shift from restrictive "protective" approaches toward individualized, evidence-based exercise prescriptions that balance risk reduction with the well-established benefits of physical activity. Key advances include updated 2024 AHA/ACC HCM management guidelines emphasizing shared decision-making, the 2024 HRS Expert Consensus on Arrhythmias in Athletes, and emerging data on myocardial microstructure abnormalities detected through advanced mapping techniques.

## Key Findings

### 1. Epidemiology of Sudden Cardiac Death in Athletes

- **Incidence**: SCD occurs at approximately 2.3 cases per year per 100,000 athletes aged 12-35 years, with cardiovascular causes accounting for 2.1 per 100,000. ([Goncalves et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.repc.2024.08.010))

- **Age and Sex Variations**: Male athletes demonstrate significantly higher SCD rates (2.6 per 100,000) compared to female athletes (1.1 per 100,000). ([Annaly Aritmologii, 2013](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/270468069))

- **Primary Causes by Age Group**:
  - **Young Athletes (<35 years)**: Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy is the leading cause, accounting for approximately one-third of all athletic deaths. Other causes include congenital coronary artery abnormalities, myocarditis, and arrhythmogenic right ventricular cardiomyopathy.
  - **Older Athletes (>35 years)**: Coronary artery disease becomes the predominant cause.

- **2024 Think Tank Findings**: The Cardiac Safety Research Consortium's January 2024 think tank emphasized that sudden cardiac arrest in the young affects children of all demographics and can strike both athletes and non-athletes. Prevention strategies are critical given the devastating impact on families and society. ([40113118](http://suppr.wilddata.cn/browses/detail/pubmed/40113118))

- **Portugal 20-Year Analysis**: A comprehensive 20-year analysis in Portugal found that competitive level and sport type significantly influence SCD incidence, highlighting the importance of targeted prevention strategies. ([Goncalves et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.repc.2024.08.010))

### 2. Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy and Exercise

- **2024 AHA/ACC HCM Management Guidelines**: Released May 2024, these guidelines represent a significant shift toward shared decision-making and more permissive exercise recommendations. Key points include:
  - Low-to-moderate intensity physical activity is now recommended for most HCM patients
  - After comprehensive risk assessment, many low-risk patients may participate in high-intensity exercise and competitive sports
  - Individualized risk stratification models guide recommendations ([严道医声, 2024](https://www.drvoice.cn/v2/article/13315))

- **Chinese Expert Consensus (2024)**: The Chinese expert consensus on HCM rehabilitation and exercise management provides specific recommendations:
  - Most HCM patients currently do not meet minimum physical activity standards
  - Evidence shows exercise improves exercise capacity, quality of life, and cardiovascular outcomes
  - Comprehensive risk assessment using echocardiography, cardiac MRI, and 24-hour Holter monitoring is recommended ([中华循环杂志, 2024](http://medsci.cn/guideline/show_article.do?id=a86b11c003559a4e))

- **Paradigm Shift**: Recent data demonstrate that moderate-intensity exercise improves functional capacity and indices of cardiac function. Continuation of competitive sports may not be associated with worse outcomes in selected patients. Athletes with implantable cardioverter defibrillators (ICDs) showed a safer profile than previously understood. ([Current Treatment Options in CV Medicine, 2021](https://link.springer.com/10.1007/s11936-021-00934-1))

- **Myocardial Microstructure Abnormalities**: Research published in JACC: Clinical Electrophysiology found that 14 of 19 patients (74%) with exercise-induced arrhythmias had microstructural abnormalities detected by ultra-high-precision intracardiac mapping, even after normal ECG, cardiac MRI, and genetic testing. These may represent sequelae of viral myocarditis or unknown genetic cardiomyopathies. ([法国波尔多大学研究团队, 2025](https://so.html5.qq.com/page/real/search_news?docid=70000021_7836876490e95252))

### 3. Long QT Syndrome and Athletic Participation

- **Chinese LQTS Guidelines (2025)**: The 2025 Chinese guidelines systemize LQTS typing, diagnosis, risk stratification, and individualized intervention strategies:
  - 13 subtypes identified (LQT1-LQT13), with LQT1, LQT2, and LQT3 comprising >90% of clinical cases
  - Beta-blockers remain the cornerstone of treatment
  - Activity restrictions are genotype-specific ([中国长QT综合征诊疗指南(2025版)](https://www.renrendoc.com/paper/510708282.html))

- **Genotype-Specific Recommendations**:
  - **LQT1**: Swimming should be avoided as it is particularly dangerous
  - **LQT2**: Sudden auditory stimuli should be avoided
  - Treatment includes beta-blockers, mexiletine, left cardiac sympathetic denervation, and ICDs when indicated ([妙佑医疗国际](https://www.mayoclinic.org/zh-hans/diseases-conditions/long-qt-syndrome/symptoms-causes/syc-20352518))

- **Exercise-Induced QT Prolongation**: Studies show that excessive physical training in adolescents may induce significant QT prolongation similar to LQTS in those without family history, asymptomatic status, and negative genotype. These abnormalities typically reverse after 3-4 months of training cessation. ([豆瓣读书](https://book.douban.com/annotation/149702970/))

### 4. Exercise-Induced Arrhythmias

- **2024 HRS Expert Consensus**: The Heart Rhythm Society, in collaboration with ACC, AHA, AMSSM, APHRS, and EHRS, released a 2024 expert consensus statement on arrhythmias in athletes covering evaluation, treatment, and return to play. ([Heart Rhythm, 2024](https://suppr.wilddata.cn/browses/detail/pubmed/38763377))

- **Age and Fitness Effects**: Research presented at ESC 2025 on 1,010 healthy adults without structural heart disease found:
  - Each 1-year increase in age associated with 8% increased atrial ectopic risk and 4% increased ventricular ectopic risk
  - Each 1-unit decrease in fitness level associated with 52% increased atrial ectopic risk
  - Higher fitness levels provide protective effects against arrhythmias ([ESC 2025](https://familydoctor.cn/news/nianling-jiasu-xinlvshichang-fengxian-jianshen-shuiping-tigong-baohu-zuoyong-192729.html))

- **Ventricular Arrhythmias in Athletes**: Data from Heart Rhythm 2025 show:
  - Exercise-related VA incidence: 0.4 per 1,000 hours of exercise
  - Non-exercise-related VA incidence: 0.01 per 1,000 hours of non-exercise time
  - Athletes with VAs had significantly higher prevalence of myocardial fibrosis (76% vs 38.3%)
  - Complex VAs in athletes are associated with higher sudden death risk ([European Journal of Preventive Cardiology, 2026](https://www.familydoctor.cn/news/zishen-nanxing-naili-yundongyuan-shixingxinlvshichang-yundong-moshi-shijian-guanlian-313285.html))

- **Commotio Cordis**: Rare but significant cause of sudden cardiac arrest in young athletes, primarily in contact sports. Caused by direct chest wall trauma at specific timing during ventricular electrical activity. Despite improved awareness, many cases go unreported. ([Cardiology in Review, 2023](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/374063428_Commotio_Cordis_A_Comprehensive_Review))

### 5. Screening for Cardiac Risk in Athletes

- **2024 Think Tank Recommendations**: The Cardiac Safety Research Consortium emphasized systematic approaches to early detection including:
  - Family history and symptom assessment
  - Physical examination
  - Standardized screening protocols
  - Emergency response planning ([40113118](http://suppr.wilddata.cn/browses/detail/pubmed/40113118))

- **ECG Abnormalities and OHCA Risk**: ESC 2024 research on 2,037,003 patients found that all common ECG abnormalities significantly increase out-of-hospital cardiac arrest risk, with hazard ratios between 1.05 and 1.06 per unit increase when adjusting for age and sex. ([ESC 2024](https://www.163.com/dy/article/JAO9P3V30552MD9C.html))

- **Screening Challenges**: International consensus on pre-competition screening remains elusive. Studies show varying efficacy of different screening approaches. The American Heart Association recommends 12-lead ECG in addition to history and physical for competitive athletes.

- **AI-Enhanced Risk Stratification**: Recent advances include:
  - MAARS (Multimodal AI for Ventricular Arrhythmoma Risk Stratification): Achieved AUC of 0.89 and 0.81 in internal and external cohorts for predicting malignant arrhythmias in HCM patients, significantly higher than current clinical guidelines (0.27-0.35). ([Nature Cardiovascular Research](https://www.vbdata.cn/intelDetail/750554))
  - Machine learning models for acquired LQTS prediction in hospitalized patients ([张萍教授团队, ESC 2025](https://so.html5.qq.com/page/real/search_news?docid=70000021_55568b4375c66452))

## Mechanisms

### Pathophysiology of Exercise-Associated Cardiac Events

1. **Hemodynamic Stress**: Intense exercise creates acute hemodynamic demands that may trigger rupture of vulnerable atherosclerotic plaques or stress existing cardiac pathology

2. **Autonomic Nervous System Activation**: Catecholamine surges during exercise can precipitate arrhythmias in susceptible individuals

3. **Electrolyte Shifts**: Exercise-induced changes in potassium, magnesium, and other electrolytes may prolong QT interval or trigger ventricular arrhythmias

4. **Myocardial Fibrosis**: Fibrotic changes in athletes, particularly endurance athletes, may create re-entry pathways for arrhythmias

5. **Genetic Susceptibility**: Inherited cardiac conditions (HCM, LQTS, ARVC) have genetic bases that may interact with exercise stress

6. **Microstructural Abnormalities**: Subtle myocardial architectural disturbances invisible to conventional imaging may underlie some exercise-induced events

## Practical Applications

### For Athletes with Known Cardiac Conditions

1. **Comprehensive Pre-Participation Evaluation**:
   - Detailed family history and symptom review
   - 12-lead ECG, echocardiography, and cardiac MRI when indicated
   - Genetic testing for suspected inherited conditions
   - Exercise stress testing for risk stratification

2. **Individualized Exercise Prescriptions**:
   - Low-to-moderate intensity aerobic exercise for most patients
   - Shared decision-making process involving athlete, family, and medical team
   - Regular reassessment of risk status

3. **Sport-Specific Considerations**:
   - Avoid swimming alone for LQT1 patients
   - Avoid sudden loud noises for LQT2 patients
   - Contact sports may be contraindicated with certain conditions (e.g., HCM with significant obstruction)

4. **Safety Provisions**:
   - On-site defibrillators at athletic events
   - Emergency action plans for coaches and staff
   - ICD consideration for high-risk patients

### For Competitive Sports Participation

1. **HCM**: Most recent guidelines permit many low-risk HCM patients to participate in competitive sports after comprehensive risk assessment and shared decision-making

2. **LQTS**: Genotype-specific activity recommendations; most patients can participate in low-to-moderate intensity activities with appropriate treatment

3. **Previous SCD Survivors**: May return to play in select cases with ICD protection and normalized cardiac function

## Conclusions

1. **SCD in athletes remains a rare but devastating event** with incidence estimates ranging from 1-3 per 100,000 athlete-years, varying significantly by age, sex, race, and sport type

2. **Paradigm shift in exercise recommendations**: Recent 2024 guidelines have moved from restrictive approaches toward individualized, evidence-based prescriptions that balance safety with the substantial benefits of physical activity

3. **HCM no longer universally contraindicates intense exercise**: Updated guidelines support moderate-intensity exercise for most HCM patients, with selected low-risk patients permitted high-intensity and competitive sports participation

4. **LQTS management is now genotype-specific**: Treatment and activity recommendations should be tailored to the specific LQTS subtype, with beta-blockers remaining foundational

5. **Advanced detection methods are improving risk stratification**: Ultra-high-precision cardiac mapping and AI-enhanced analysis are identifying subtle abnormalities previously invisible to conventional screening

6. **Screening efficacy remains debated**: While ECG screening shows promise, international consensus on optimal protocols has not been established. Family history, physical examination, and symptom assessment remain fundamental

7. **Fitness provides protective effects**: Regular physical activity reduces arrhythmia risk, supporting the importance of maintaining exercise habits even in at-risk populations

8. **Emergency preparedness is essential**: On-site defibrillation and organized emergency response systems are critical components of any athletic program
